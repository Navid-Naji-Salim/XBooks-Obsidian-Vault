I actually think this is a great idea—but I wouldn't integrate AI just for the sake of saying "it has AI." Employers see through that pretty quickly.

Knowing X Books, your target audience (software companies in Erbil), and the fact that this is meant to be a polished foundation rather than a bloated accounting suite, I'd add one or two AI features that feel genuinely useful.

Here are the ones I'd rank highest.

1. AI Invoice & Bill Assistant (my favorite) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

On the invoice or bill page, add an "Ask AI" button.

Examples:

"Create an invoice for a website development project worth $2,500 with a 30% upfront payment."

"Add monthly hosting for one year."

"Generate a bill for office furniture."


The AI fills:

customer/vendor

line items

quantities

descriptions

notes


The user reviews everything before saving.

This immediately demonstrates:

AI integration

prompt engineering

structured output

practical business value


without changing how the accounting system works.


---

2. AI Business Insights ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

On Home:

> "This month, invoice revenue increased 18%."



> "You have 5 unpaid invoices worth $12,400."



> "Vendor spending is unusually high compared to last month."



> "Customer ABC has become your highest-paying client."



Instead of charts talking...

...the AI talks.

That's modern.


---

3. Natural-language search ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆

Instead of only:

> Search invoices...



Allow:

> unpaid invoices from last month



or

> invoices for Ahmed over $500



or

> customers that haven't bought anything recently



It feels incredibly modern despite being relatively lightweight.


---

4. AI report summaries ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Inside Reports:

Instead of just generating a PDF:

Add

> ✨ Summarize Report



Then:

> Revenue increased 14% this month.



> Expenses remained stable.



> Office rent represented 27% of operating expenses.



> Net profit improved mainly because marketing costs decreased.



This is exactly the kind of feature people expect from accounting software over the next few years.


---

5. Receipt OCR ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆

Upload a receipt.

AI extracts:

vendor

date

amount

tax

line items


Then creates a draft bill.

Very impressive.


---

What I would NOT add

I would avoid things like:

chatbot floating in the corner

"How can I help?"

AI writing emails

random GPT integration with no accounting purpose


Those often feel tacked on.


---

If I were reviewing your portfolio...

The feature that would make me stop and think, "This developer understands where business software is going," would be this combination:

✨ Ask AI on invoices/bills to generate structured drafts from natural language.

✨ AI Insights on the Home dashboard that explain what the numbers mean instead of just displaying them.


Those two features complement the philosophy of X Books perfectly. They don't try to replace accounting—they reduce repetitive work and help users understand their data. That makes the application feel like a modern accounting platform rather than simply an accounting CRUD app.

And because X Books is positioned as a polished foundation that can later become "Acme Books," "Contoso Books," or another branded accounting system, these AI capabilities reinforce that story. They show that the foundation isn't just reusable—it's built with contemporary software expectations in mind.
