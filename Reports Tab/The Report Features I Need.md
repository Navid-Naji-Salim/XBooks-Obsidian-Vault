I think the **Reports** tab is where XBooks can really feel like an accounting application instead of just a CRUD app.

The key is to avoid trying to recreate Zoho's dozens of reports. For your project, you only need the reports that naturally summarize the data you're already collecting.

I'd include these:

### 1. Profit & Loss (most important) ⭐

Shows:

- Total Sales
    
- Total Purchases
    
- Gross Profit
    

This is the report I'd expect first.

---

### 2. Sales Report

Summarizes invoices:

- Total invoices
    
- Paid vs Unpaid
    
- Revenue over a selected period
    

---

### 3. Purchases Report

Summarizes bills:

- Total bills
    
- Paid vs Unpaid
    
- Total spending
    

---

### 4. Customers Report

Shows things like:

- Total customers
    
- Total sales per customer
    
- Outstanding balances
    

---

### 5. Vendors Report

The mirror of Customers:

- Total vendors
    
- Total purchases per vendor
    
- Outstanding balances
    

---

## What I'd avoid

Don't add things like:

- Balance Sheet
    
- Cash Flow
    
- Trial Balance
    
- Journal
    
- General Ledger
    
- Inventory Valuation
    

Those are great features, but they dramatically increase complexity and require much more accounting infrastructure than XBooks currently has.

---

### My recommendation

I'd actually make the Reports page a **dashboard**, not just a list of links.

At the top:

- Revenue
    
- Expenses
    
- Profit
    
- Outstanding Receivables
    
- Outstanding Payables
    

Then below, show cards for:

- Profit & Loss
    
- Sales Report
    
- Purchases Report
    
- Customers Report
    
- Vendors Report
    

When you click a card, it opens that report.

I think that's the right balance for XBooks. It showcases your ability to aggregate and visualize business data without trying to implement an entire enterprise accounting suite.
